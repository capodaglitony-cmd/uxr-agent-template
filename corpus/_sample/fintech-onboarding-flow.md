# Case: Fintech onboarding flow framing (fictional)

**Note:** This is a fabricated case story used as sample content for the uxr-agent-template. It is not based on a real organization or project.

## Situation

A consumer fintech was launching a new investing product targeted at first-time investors. Onboarding completion was the primary KPI for the launch. The PM had a hunch that "first-time investor anxiety" was the key barrier, but the team disagreed about whether the friction was emotional (anxiety) or informational (people didn't understand what they were signing up for).

## Task

Define the actual problem before designing the onboarding flow. The team had budget for one round of generative research and pressure to start design within five weeks. Available stimulus material included two competitor onboarding screen recordings and an internal alpha prototype of three sample screens.

## Action

Routed this to a Define / Direction cascade. Recommended a co-creation workshop format combined with concept testing on the early-stage prototype. Three workshops, six participants each, recruited across age (22-34) and prior investing experience (none, dabbled in crypto, used a robo-advisor before).

Workshop structure: 30 minutes shared baseline (what the product was supposed to do), 45 minutes paired ideation on "what would make you actually start using this," 45 minutes reaction to the alpha prototype, 30 minutes synthesis. Workshops surfaced a third hypothesis nobody had considered: the friction wasn't anxiety or comprehension, it was *trust signaling* — participants couldn't tell whether the product was a "real" financial institution or a startup that might disappear.

## Result

The trust hypothesis wasn't on anybody's bingo card going in. Participants kept returning to specific signals: was this FDIC insured, who held the money, what happened if the company went under. The alpha prototype mentioned none of these.

Recommended a redesign priority shift: trust signals (FDIC notice, custodian disclosure, regulatory filings) move to onboarding screen 1, anxiety-reducing framing ("you can start with $5") to screen 2, comprehension scaffolding to screen 3. The original design had these in reverse order.

Design team adopted the reframe. Onboarding completion at six-month launch hit 64 percent vs. an internal target of 52 percent. The PM reflected later that the original framing — "anxiety vs. comprehension" — would have led to a flow that solved neither real problem.

## Methodological notes

- Co-creation workshops worked here because the team had a hunch but couldn't pin it down. A purely evaluative method (usability testing on the alpha) would have produced "yes, this is confusing" without surfacing the trust frame.
- Three workshops, six participants each (n=18) hit thematic saturation by workshop two on the trust signal pattern. We ran the third anyway as a confirmation.
- The PM and Designer disagreed productively in the synthesis: PM wanted to ship trust signals as a banner; Designer wanted them woven into the screen-1 copy. The Engineer flagged that FDIC/custodian disclosure had legal-review constraints that limited copy flexibility. All three perspectives ended up in the final spec.
